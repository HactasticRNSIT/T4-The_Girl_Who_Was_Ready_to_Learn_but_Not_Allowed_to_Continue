Low Attendance Detected — Barriers Given by Society
"These are the barriers that society places on girl children, preventing them from attending school regularly."
Society will not allow her out during periods
Many girls miss 4–5 school days every month due to social taboos around menstruation. Communities often restrict girls from leaving home, severely impacting continuity of education.
Household work and responsibilities
Girls are routinely burdened with cooking, cleaning, fetching water, and sibling care — duties rarely assigned to brothers — keeping them home instead of in the classroom.
Lack of sanitation facilities in school
Schools without private, clean, gender-separate toilets make adolescent girls uncomfortable and unsafe — a leading cause of dropout, especially after puberty.
Seasonal agricultural work with family
During sowing and harvesting seasons, girls are pulled from school to assist with farm labour. This recurring absenteeism often leads to permanent dropout over time.
